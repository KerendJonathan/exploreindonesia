Indonesian Tradional Spices

Ceylon Clove (Cengkeh Celyon)

Prized for its distinct flavour and aroma, and also for its medicinal qualities, cloves are the immature buds of the evergreen tree Syzygium aromaticum , also known as Eugenia caryophyllus of the Myrtaceae family. A fully grown clove tree is about 15-20 metres tall and has smooth grey bark. Ceylon Clove is notably richer in oil than the clove varieties produced elsewhere in the world.

Powder and Sticks of Cinnamon (Kayu Manis)

"Cinnamon is a spice that comes from the inner bark of the tropical Cinnamomum tree (colloquially known as the cinnamon tree), sold as rolled quills (cinnamon sticks) or ground into a fine powder. Indonesia was the only source of cinnamon, kept secret by Netherlands traders who wished to monopolize the market. Even now, cinnamon in its purest formcan only be found here.

Black Pepper (Lada Hitam)

In Indonesia, pepper or the woody perennial evergreen climbing vine, Piper nigrum, is cultivated over an area of 32800 hectares and majorly in the districts of Matale, Kandy, Kegalle, and Kurunegala where it’s grown either as a mono-crop or a mixed crop in coconut and tea plantations using live or dead stands as support. It’s also ideal for home gardens. Today, pure origin pepper enjoys a huge demand internationally, and the public is keener to discover spices that originate from a specific “terroir” just like wines. Ceylon Pepper is mainly exported to Netherlands, Germany and the USA and enjoys a growing demand from Australia and Canada.

Nutmeg Seeds (Biji Pala)

Nutmeg seeds are the seeds of the nutmeg plant (Myristica fragrans), which is known for its distinctive aroma and flavor. Nutmeg is used as a spice in cooking and food processing. Nutmeg has a warm and slightly sweet taste, which makes it often used in sweet and savory dishes.

Indonesian Foods And Drinks

Rendang

Rendang is a traditional Indonesian dish that is world-famous. It is made from beef cooked with coconut milk and a variety of spices, such as lemongrass, galangal, chili, turmeric, and more. The process of cooking rendang takes a long time until the meat becomes tender and the spices are fully absorbed.

Soto Santan

Soto Santan is a delicious and flavorful Indonesian dish. It is a thick soup made from coconut milk (shredded coconut) cooked with various spices, such as lemongrass, galangal, ginger, shallots and turmeric. The cooking process with coconut milk gives it an appetizing texture and rich flavor.

Surabi

Surabi is a traditional Indonesian cake made from rice flour, coconut milk and sugar. The cake is soft and sweet, often served with grated coconut or brown sugar. A favorite as a snack or dessert.

Sate

Satay is a traditional Indonesian food that has become a worldwide favorite. It consists of pieces of meat (such as chicken, beef, mutton or pork) that are thinly sliced and skewered using bamboo or metal skewers. The skewered meat is then grilled over charcoal coals or a grill, giving it a distinctive flavor that tantalizes the taste buds.

Indonesian Industrial Goods

Green Tea

Indonesian green tea is made from Camellia sinensis leaves without undergoing full oxidation. Processed by heating or drying, this tea contains polyphenols and catechins that are beneficial for health. It tastes fresh, light and slightly bitter. Indonesian green tea holds a special place in the country's tea culture and has recognized health value.

Gold

Gold Indonesia is the production and export of gold, which is an important part of the country's mining industry and economy. Indonesia has several gold mines that contribute to economic growth, but also face environmental and social challenges. It is important to manage gold wisely for sustainability and community welfare..

Pottery

Pottery making is a handcrafted art of clay. Clay is shaped, dried, decorated, and baked in a kiln until it becomes a hard, beautiful ceramic. The process requires skill and love for the art to produce high-value pieces.

Batik Clothes

Indonesian batik is a unique and beautiful traditional art in the process of dyeing cloth. The batik method involves using hot wax to prevent the color from spreading to certain parts of the fabric. After coloring and creating intricate patterns, the wax is melted to reveal the stunning design. Indonesian batik is a cherished cultural heritage, recognized by UNESCO as a Heritage of Humanity in 2009. Indonesia's signature batik cloth is considered a symbol of pride and beauty of its traditional art.

Indonesian Arts and crafts

Anyaman Rotan

Rattan weaving is a traditional Indonesian handicraft art using strong and flexible rattan fibers. The products are diverse, such as chairs, tables, baskets, and accessories. The weaving process requires high precision and skill. This art has become part of Indonesian culture and has been recognized as a valuable cultural heritage.

Jars

A jug is a traditional Indonesian clay or ceramic vessel. It is used to store and serve water or drinks. It has a large round shape with a long, curved neck. It symbolizes Indonesia's rich and precious ceramic culture and art.

Traditional Indonesian Dancer

Indonesian traditional dance is a rich and diverse cultural heritage. Each region in Indonesia has its own signature dance, reflecting the diversity of local culture, customs and history. Traditional Indonesian dance features elegant and meaningful movements, often depicting mythical stories, celebrations, or daily activities.

Wooden architecture and wood-carving

Indonesian carvings are a beautiful and intricate traditional art using wood, stone, and other materials. The motifs are inspired by nature, culture, and religious beliefs. These carvings have cultural and religious significance, preserved as an integral part of Indonesia's cultural heritage.
